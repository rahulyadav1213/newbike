About Me

"Life is like Riding a Motorcycle"

When you build an almost completely bespoke motorbike essentially at home, then turn it into a race winner, a national championship winner, and set a number of world records, then you file that under “Score One for the Little Guy.” That being said, there is nothing about John Britten’s ideas and achievements that can be called “little.” Britten may have been a dreamer, but he had the wherewithal, the technical ability and the pure determination to turn his dreams into reality. He was also very much able to recruit people to help him do it if needed. This was all the case when it came to the production and implementation of the V1000 from 1991 to 1998. In 1992, motorcycle journalist Alan Cathcart called the V1000 “the most sophisticated, the most technically advanced motorcycle in the world today.” High praise, especially when you consider that it came from New Zealand – a country not known for motorcycle manufacturing. The story of Britten and his V1000 was one of hard work and much trial and error, but boy was it ever rewarded. Britten died at the too-young age of 45 from cancer, but left a legacy of ingenuity and engineering that is valuable not only to New Zealanders, but to the entire motorcycling world.
